## Emotional Angle

Pick the dominant angle and write to it. Strong launches usually land on one of:

- **A glimmer of hope** — a hopeful take in a noisy, cynical category.
- **Pointing at something broken** — name what is broken and say we are the ones fixing it.
- **A category reframe** — a genuinely new take that changes how people think about the whole space.

## Copy Guardrails

The 2026 do-not-do list (checked deterministically against the fenced post copy):

- No hashtags. No emojis carrying the message. No link in the main post — link goes in the first reply.
- No generic "we shipped X" copy that earns an "okay" and a scroll. Make it lovable or hateable.
- Lead with the hook/POV, not a preamble. No nuance in the post — save nuance for the replies.
- No "congrats!" seeded replies — every pre-briefed reply adds a distinct angle.

### Launch-Day Timing Engine

One principle: make it feel organic — layer amplification gradually, starting closest to your ICP.

- **Gate 1 — Let it breathe (0–60 min):** post live, do nothing externally, reply to every comment,
  watch likes/min, reply quality, retweet pace. Strong velocity → proceed; weak → fix before amplifying.
- **Gate 2 — First wave (hours 1–2):** deploy 10–20 influencers staggered in 30-min windows, biggest
  ICP first; each retweets the main post, runs its own giveaway, and works its comments.
- **Gate 3 — Full deployment + live adjustment (hours 2–3):** add theme pages / Discord where momentum
  builds, hold back where it slips, coordinate cross-engagement.
- **Reply to everything for 48 hours** — author-reply chains are the strongest signal; substantive replies, not "thanks".

## Phase 6 — Follow-Up Window

The momentum window after a viral post boosts what you post inside it. Don't waste it.

- Day 2: meme or short AI-generated video riffing on the launch.
- Day 3: deep dive on one feature.
- Day 4: founder story / behind-the-scenes.
- Week 2: results, testimonials, early traction.

## Sourcing And Ranking

The platform's two signals (the agency's working model, not official docs), used as a planning lens:

- **Sourcing — will it be shown?** Retweets qualify the post for the For You feed. This is why every giveaway requires a retweet.
- **Ranking — how high?** Reply chains where you respond back; conversation = time on site. Every author reply creates a new chain.
