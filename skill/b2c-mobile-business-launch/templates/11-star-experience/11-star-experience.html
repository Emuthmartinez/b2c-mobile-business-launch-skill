Design the unreasonable end-to-end experience, draw the line of feasibility, then ship the smallest V1 slice that still feels worth retelling.

Star Ladder

Refund me
1
The app breaks trust.

The promise fails, the user feels misled, or recovery is unclear.

Emotion: regret. Lesson: define what must never ship.
It technically works
2
The task completes with friction.

The user waits, guesses, retries, or wonders whether the result is trustworthy.

Emotion: tolerance. Lesson: remove avoidable drag.
Expected experience
5
The app does what it says.

The baseline product promise works, but nobody would describe it as special.

Emotion: neutral. Lesson: this is the floor.
Line Of Feasibility: V1 should usually live between 6 and 7 stars.
Better than expected
6
The product reflects the user.

Inputs come back as a useful next step, clear explanation, or confidence-building result.

Emotion: relief. Lesson: polish plus relevance creates trust.
Way beyond
7
The next best step feels prepared.

The user sees a result, recommendation, or path that feels context-aware and personal.

Emotion: "made for me." Lesson: this is the V1 magic candidate.
Impossible concierge
10
A human team manually creates the perfect result.

Every detail is anticipated, customized, checked, and delivered with no user effort.

Emotion: amazement. Lesson: find the service blueprint.
Absurd extreme
11
The problem disappears like magic.

The outcome is so complete and surprising that the user wants to tell everyone.

Emotion: evangelism. Lesson: name the real ambition.
